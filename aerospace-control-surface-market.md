<p><p>Aerospace control surfaces include various types of devices that assist in controlling and maneuvering an aircraft. Flaps are located on the wings and help increase lift or drag during takeoff and landing. Slats are movable surfaces located on the leading edge of wings that enhance lift at lower speeds. Spoilers are used to decrease lift on the wings and increase drag during landing or to improve maneuverability. Ailerons are found on the wings and enable the aircraft to roll left or right. Elevators control the aircraft's pitch movement, while rudders control its yaw movement.</p></p>
