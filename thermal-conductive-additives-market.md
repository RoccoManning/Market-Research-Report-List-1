<p><p>The thermal conductive additives market can be divided into two major types: thermoplastic additives and thermosetting additives. Thermoplastic additives refer to materials that can be repeatedly melted and solidified, such as polyethylene or polypropylene. These additives can enhance the thermal conductivity of thermoplastic polymers, making them suitable for applications in various industries. On the other hand, thermosetting additives are materials that undergo irreversible chemical reactions when heated, resulting in a rigid and insoluble structure. These additives can improve the thermal conductivity of thermosetting polymers like epoxy or phenolic resins, making them useful in heat dissipation applications.</p></p>
