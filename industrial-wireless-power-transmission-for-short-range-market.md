<p><p>Industrial wireless power transmission for short-range refers to the transfer of electrical energy without the need for physical wires. There are two main market types in this domain: electromagnetic induction technology and magnetic field coupling technology. Electromagnetic induction technology uses a primary coil to generate a magnetic field, which induces a current in a secondary coil, thus transferring power. Magnetic field coupling technology involves using magnetic fields to transfer power between two coils that are in close proximity. Both technologies enable efficient and convenient power transfer over short distances in various industrial applications.</p></p>
